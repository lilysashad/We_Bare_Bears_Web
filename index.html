What is We Bare Bears?

We Bare Bears is an American cartoon program about three bear brothers. Each episode is a wild adventure across San Francisco, California.

Characters

The oldest bear brother is Grizz. Originally from Canada, he is a grizzly bear that acts as the team leader. Grizz is the most determined and brave of the bunch.

The second bear brother is Panda. He had escaped from a conservatory in China. Panda is timid, and his hobbies include going on his phone and watching Japanese anime.

The youngest bear brother is Ice Bear. Ice Bear is a polar bear from the Arctic. He rarely speaks, but he is incredibly intelligent, and thoughtful of his brothers.

Why Do I Like We Bare Bears?

We Bare Bears is such a wholesome program that centers around brotherly love and offers a diverse perspective on life in San Francisco.

There are numerous instances where the bears are unabashedly affectionate, and support each other like a real family would. For instance,
Ice Bear's expression of love manifests in his cooking, or love language in food. Throughout the show, Ice Bear prepares appropriate meals
for Grizz and Panda- meat and vegetables, respectively. In Losing Ice, Grizz and Panda take Ice Bear's hard work for granted, causing
Ice Bear to feel undervalued, so he takes up a job at a local Japanese grill. In Ice Bear's absence, Grizz and Panda realize their selfishness and
finally apologize to Ice Bear, expressing their gratitude for his efforts to care for the family. Aside from Ice Bear's cooking, Grizz continuously
supports Panda's pursuits in relationships and passions through various schemes. Although they often fall through, Panda appreciates his brother's
attempts. In Panda's Sneeze, Panda becomes an Internet sensation when a video of his cute sneeze garners attention. During a "cute-off" (a competition
in which one competitor must act more cute than the other) with another Internet sensation, Grizz repeatedly expresses his excitement and pride in
Panda for having stepped out of his comfort zone. In addition, Grizz gives Panda moral support and pays him compliments when the latter faces a
particular tough spot in the competition.

Multiple episodes of the We Bare Bears depict different kinds of people in San Francisco. Since it is a diverse area, sometimes these characters are
chilling in the background or actually have roles in that particular episode. For example, side character Chloe Park is a 10-year old Korean-American
academic prodigy that hangs out with the bears, and some episodes respectfully reference her culture. Additionally, the bears occasionally venture into
Chinatown and the street decorations/shops accurately reflect the area (based on that one time I visited San Francisco's Chinatown). Lastly, Panda's hobbies
offer viewers insight on popular aspects of Japanese and Korean culture- anime and K-pop, respectively. In particular, Panda's Birthday features a cameo from
talented K-pop boy group Monsta X and producers did not shy away from letting members speak their native language.
